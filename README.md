### Project Overview and Data Rectification

This project implements an end-to-end Customer 360 Analytical and Machine Learning Pipeline designed to solve systemic operational inefficiencies and high customer attrition on a multi-vendor marketplace platform. By integrating fragmented transaction records, logistics timelines, and unstructured Portuguese customer reviews, raw data was cleaned, deduplicated at the unique order level to remove inflation bias, and transformed into distinct entity-centric features. Exploratory Data Analysis validated historical sales velocity milestones, such as a major performance zenith in November 2017 driven by Black Friday campaigns, while a unified box-plot framework successfully profiled extreme logistical and pricing outliers across the platform catalog.

### Feature Engineering and Lifecycle Segmentation

Feature engineering was used to standardize financial and retention indicators across the dataset. A consolidated target metric, Total_Revenue, was created by combining item base prices with freight values, while fulfillment latency was mapped by calculating delivery day intervals. To construct the customer profiles, database tables were aggregated into recency, frequency, and monetary lifecycle features, which successfully isolated active loyalists from high-risk, single-purchase customer profiles. Additionally, regional buyer demand footprints were overlaid against registered vendor locations to engineer a geographic demand-to-supply ratio feature, uncovering critical spatial gaps where high consumer volumes lacked a localized merchant presence.

### Empirical Insights and Strategic Interventions

The pipeline text-mining analytics proved that over half of negative platform sentiment stems directly from shipping carrier delays rather than manufacturing defects, with customer satisfaction collapsing once transit timelines exceed an 18-day threshold, particularly for bulk categories like Bed, Bath and Table. These data-driven discoveries support targeted marketplace interventions over costly, site-wide promotions. Key recommendations include localizing logistics networks with private couriers for high-risk categories, deploying automated win-back campaigns with free priority shipping upgrades for dormant premium buyers, targeting merchant recruitment in high-demand geographic gaps, and enforcing an automated storefront guardrail that freezes listings for high-volume products that fall below a 2.5-star average.
